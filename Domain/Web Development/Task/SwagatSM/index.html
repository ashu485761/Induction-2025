Bestseller

"Abc of Electrical Engineering" by Theraja B L and Theraja A K

“Abc of Electrical Engineering” Book Review: This book is specifically designed for students in their first year of B.E., B.Tech., and A.M.I.E. programs. It covers a wide range of topics including electric current and resistance, DC network theorems, electrostatics, and capacitors. It provides detailed explanations on magnetism, magnetic circuits, and complex algebra. The book also explores AC fundamentals, AC series circuits, parallel AC circuits, motors, and more. To aid in understanding, the book incorporates numerous diagrams to illustrate the concepts presented.

"Higher Engineering Mathematics" by Grewal B S

“Higher Engineering Mathematics” Book Review: The author spent many years researching and wrote this book. It teaches Complex Analysis with vector algebra and has lots of examples that are solved and unsolved. There are also exercises at the end of each chapter. The book is for students who are preparing for the GATE exam or studying the subject in college.

"Simplified Engineering for Architects and Builders" by James Ambrose and Patrick Tripeny

“Simplified Engineering for Architects and Builders” Book Review: This book introduces the basic concepts of designing structures for buildings. It covers fundamental functions of structures, wood construction, steel construction, concrete construction, and structural systems for buildings. Diagrams, tables with data, and graphical representations are provided for better understanding. At the end of each chapter, various problems and solutions are included for students to practice. This book is suitable for candidates studying architecture, building construction, and civil engineering. It is written in simple language and is easy to understand.
